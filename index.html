Patrick

I am an applied physics graduate from The Technical University of Kenya. I have specialized in materials science, with focus on computational materials modelling. Specifically, I have extensively used SIESTA and other Python based packages in studying materials. From 2017, I developed a strong passion in machine learning and its related fields. Over the years, I have extensively interacted with these technologies using languages such as Python and R. I have also been privileged to have been selected as a finalist in a competitive Msc Research stay at the University of Turku, for a study that significantly relied on both DFT and machine learning.

Education

The Technical University of Kenya

Bachelor of Technology Technical and Applied Physics • July 2021

Majored in Materials science. For my thesis, I studied the interplay of lattice distortions and bands near the Fermi Level of ATiO3 using ab-initio methods. The manuscript for this work can be found as a preprint here https://arxiv.org/abs/2009.02529. This work is still in progress using different DFT approaches. I also collaborated on an ab-initio study investigating the origin of band inversion in topoligical BiSe. This work can be found here https://aip.scitation.org/doi/full/10.1063/5.0022525 .

Work

Upepo Technologies

Data Science Intern • November 2020 - February 2021

Upepo Technology is a leading IoT firm in Nairobi that deals with the installation and maintenance of smart meters as a substitute for the traditional manual meters. My primary responsibilities included developing and maintaining the firm's dashboards, aiding in trouble shooting and diagnosing faulty meters using the meters' electronic data, developing predictive models for the company's clients as well as analyzing the firm's historic data. One of the key highlights of my work was developing a time-series model that predicted household water consumption for the next three months. The regression model was evaluated against actual water consumption for residents in a Kenyan town and the resulting error metric was found to be significantly low thus showing the deployability of the model.

The Catholic University of Eastern Africa

Graduate Assistant Intern, Department of Natural Sciences • November 2020 - February 2021

Computational methods of materials modelling are becoming increasingly popular as an alternative to experimental methods. At the Catholic University of Eastern Africa, my responsibilities mainly revolved around performing electronic calculations for different kinds of materials as well as processing the results of the calculations. This work was mostly done using SIESTA code.I also led various peer interaction sessions where we shared knowledge on different concepts of Density Functional Theory. Such sessions included topics like pseudo-potential generation and testing as well as creating basis sets.
